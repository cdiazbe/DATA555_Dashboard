# Perinatal Diet and Preeclampsia Dashboard

## Overview
This project presents an interactive dashboard exploring the relationship between dietary patterns during pregnancy and the risk of preeclampsia, a hypertensive disorder with important maternal and neonatal health implications.

## Research Objective
This study aims to compare dietary patterns derived using multiple clustering algorithms and assess whether differences in these algorithms lead to different conclusions about preeclampsia risk.

## Key Findings
- Dietary patterns and their defining food groups differed across clustering algorithms, with limited consistency in how individuals were grouped.
- Estimated risk differences for preeclampsia varied by algorithm, with some suggesting lower risk and others higher risk across clusters. However, all confidence intervals included the null, indicating no statistically significant associations.
- Overall, findings highlight that both dietary pattern characterization and estimated associations with preeclampsia are sensitive to the choice of clustering algorithm.

## Real-World Impact
This dashboard shows that results about diet and preeclampsia can change depending on the clustering method used. This highlights the need for careful methodological choices in nutritional epidemiology and more cautious interpretation of findings.
